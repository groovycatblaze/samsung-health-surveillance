Statistical Analysis

The paradox of
malnutrition & obesity

States with high child anaemia don't uniformly have low obesity. The "double burden" hypothesis plays out across Indian states — anaemia and obesity coexist in the same geographies.

Anaemia vs Obesity — Women

Negative correlation (r ≈ −0.42). Southern and coastal states cluster in low-anaemia, high-obesity quadrant. BIMARU states dominate high anaemia territory.

Hypertension vs Blood Sugar

Strong positive correlation (r ≈ 0.72). States managing one NCD tend to face the other — suggesting shared lifestyle and metabolic risk factors.

Project Methods

Built in three phases

A reproducible EDA pipeline from raw NFHS-5 CSV to interactive geographic visualizations.

01

Data Ingestion & Cleaning

Downloaded from Kaggle via kagglehub. Standardized column names, pivoted sub-indicator rows into a per-state feature matrix, coerced numerics, handled special characters and missing values.

pandas kagglehub numpy
02

EDA & Statistical Analysis

Computed Pearson correlation matrix across all 8 indicators. Generated scatter plots with OLS regression lines. Identified the gender obesity gap (Women − Men BMI differential) as a derived feature.

matplotlib numpy.polyfit correlation matrix
03

Geographic Visualization

Choropleth maps via Folium + CartoDB Positron tiles. Circle markers at state capitals encode blood sugar and anaemia rates. Saved as standalone HTML for deployment.

folium GeoJSON CartoDB
